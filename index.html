Integria

In the INTEGRIA project, we focused on object picking tasks, but our software can be adapted to other kinds of manipulation tasks. To do so, we would have to re-implement the specificities of the new task environment (type of robot, scene components,...), the objective of the task (how to reward/punish the AI for what it does), and potentially the new type of actions of the robot. Then our software could be run, simulating dozens of robots trying to perform the target task, and learning to get better at it over time.

Our software is able to train robots to perform manipulation tasks in a simulated environment, through Deep Reinforcement Learning. Using a depth camera as a perception system, the control AIs trained in simulation can then be directly transferred in the real world, with the same level of performance.

Ragi

The RAGI system combines robots, cameras, screens and above all a lot of algorithmic calculations in order to recognize people's faces and guide them. The prototype is installed in the Montefiore Institute building, so that researchers have a system that has been tested and improved in real conditions of use.

This system is a demonstrator whose purpose is to to popularize the skills in Artificial Intelligence of the of the Montefiore Institute of the ULiège, and more particularly the facial facial recognition and robotics.
